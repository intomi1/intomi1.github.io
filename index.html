Braintrust Creative

A clear, respectful way to make stories stronger.

The Braintrust Method is a structured creative feedback process for writers, directors, producers and teams. It is not a committee and it does not replace the author’s voice. Its purpose is to reveal where the story is alive, where it is not yet clear, and which decisions would make the project more precise.

Core Question What is the human question at the center of the story?
Clarity What is working, what is confusing, and what feels emotionally untrue?
Trust Direct feedback without performance, ego or panic.
Next Steps Concrete priorities for scenes, characters, structure, tone and pitch.

“The best feedback does not tell the artist what to do. It helps them recognize what the work is already asking for.”

For whom

Feature films, animation projects, series concepts, novels, short stories, treatments, pitch decks and early-stage creative material that needs honest but constructive development.

Writing

Literary work behind the screen work.

My work as a writer remains closely connected to my work as a producer. Literature is where I test voice, memory, character, rhythm and moral ambiguity before they become larger narrative worlds.

I have published several books, as well as prose, essays and children’s stories through Liget and Szitakötő. These texts often move around family memory, childhood, language, absurdity, hidden motives and the strange point where everyday life becomes symbolic.

This literary background informs how I approach films, animation and immersive content: through character, detail, atmosphere and the unsaid.

Approach

The story must remain human.

Every project begins with a person under pressure. What do they want? What are they hiding? What are they afraid to admit? Once that is clear, structure, tone, visuals and production decisions can serve the same purpose.

“I help protect the human center of a story while making it strong enough to develop, pitch and produce.”
